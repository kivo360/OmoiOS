# OmoiOS Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> Last updated: January 2026

## Primary ICP: The Overwhelmed Engineering Leader

### Who They Are

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Title** | Engineering Manager, VP of Engineering, CTO |
| **Company Size** | 20-100 engineers (scaling startups, mid-market) |
| **Funding** | Series A-C funded OR profitable mid-sized business |
| **Budget** | Has runway/budget for tooling ($5K-50K/year range) |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS, Fintech, Healthcare Tech, E-commerce platforms |

### Secondary ICP: Agency Technical Leadership

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Title** | Technical Director, Agency CTO, Development Lead |
| **Company Type** | Digital agency, software consultancy |
| **Team Size** | 10-50 developers juggling 5-15 client projects |
| **Pain** | Context switching, parallel project delivery, client deadlines |

---

## The Pain (Why They Buy)

### Trigger Events
These moments create urgency:

1. **Just missed a deadline** - Client/stakeholder trust damaged
2. **Lost a deal to a faster competitor** - "They shipped it in 3 weeks, we quoted 3 months"
3. **Key engineer quit** - Took 6 months of context with them
4. **Board pressure** - "Why is velocity dropping as we hire more?"
5. **Hiring freeze** - Backlog keeps growing, headcount doesn't

### The Quantifiable Problem

| Pain Point | Cost |
|------------|------|
| Senior engineer salary | $150-250K/year |
| Time to hire | 3-6 months |
| Onboarding to productivity | 3-6 months |
| Context switching overhead | 20-40% productivity loss |
| Missed deadline penalties | $10K-100K+ per incident |
| Technical debt accumulation | 2-3x future development cost |

### Emotional State
- Frustrated: "We keep hiring but don't ship faster"
- Anxious: "One resignation away from missing our roadmap"
- Skeptical: "AI coding tools make junior-level mistakes"
- Desperate: "I'll try anything at this point"

## Cold Email Strategy

### Research Before Outreach
1. Find their tech blog / engineering blog
2. Check their GitHub org activity
3. Look at their job postings (what skills = what problems)
4. Find interviews/podcasts with their engineering leaders

### Email Framework (Ogilvy-style: short, clear, natural)

**Subject:** [Specific observation about their company]

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

[One sentence showing you researched them - specific to their situation]

[One sentence about the pain you solve - framed as a question]

[One sentence offer - specific, low-commitment]

[Clear CTA - one action]

[Signature]
```

**Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Saw you're hiring 12 engineers while also shipping a new payments integration by Q2.

What if you could ship that integration without waiting for the new hires to ramp up?

I built a tool that turns specs into working code - happy to show you on a real ticket from your backlog. 15 min, no pitch.

Worth a look?

Kevin
```

### Follow-up Cadence
- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 3: Bump ("Floating this up")
- Day 7: New angle/value add
- Day 14: Breakup email ("Closing the loop")

## Validation Checklist

Before scaling outreach, validate with 10 conversations:

- [ ] Do they confirm the pain exists?
- [ ] Is the pain acute enough to pay for?
- [ ] What's their current workaround?
- [ ] What would make them switch?
- [ ] What's their budget authority?
- [ ] What's their buying process?
- [ ] What would "proof" look like to them?
